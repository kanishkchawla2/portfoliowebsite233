Empirical Study of WACC for NSE-Listed Companies

Mar 2026 Valuation

Project Overview

Empirical study of Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) for all NSE-listed companies. Cross-sectional analysis across 11 sectors, 50+ industries, and 16 credit rating categories. Three WACC estimates computed per company using Daily, Weekly, and Monthly beta frequencies.

Key Metrics: Risk-Free Rate: 6.67% | Equity Risk Premium: 5.23% | Market: NSE India

Why WACC Matters

  • WACC represents the minimum return a company must earn to satisfy its capital providers
  • Acts as a risk mirror — higher WACC signals higher perceived risk by the market
  • Central to DCF valuation, capital budgeting, and investment decision-making
  • Higher WACC → Higher Risk → Higher Required Return

Methodology

  • Retrieved complete NSE stock universe using nselib
  • Fetched historical price data and financials using yfinance
  • Beta calculated across three frequencies: Daily (1Y), Weekly (3Y), Monthly (5Y)
  • Cost of Equity computed via CAPM: Ke = Rf + β × ERP
  • Cost of Debt derived using Damodaran's Interest Coverage Ratio → Credit Rating → Default Spread framework
  • Capital weights based on market values of equity and debt
  • Post-processing and analysis done in Excel using FILTER, INDEX, LOOKUPS, and CHOOSECOLS

Tech Stack

  • Python: pandas, yfinance, nselib
  • Excel: advanced formulas for structuring, mapping, and computation
  • AI-assisted coding used to accelerate data pipeline development
  • Entire data collection and structuring completed in under an hour

Key Empirical Findings

  • WACC Across Sectors: Real Estate highest at 13.67%, Healthcare lowest at 11.52%. Spread of ~215 basis points. Sectors with higher leverage and cyclicality showed elevated WACC.
  • WACC Across Industries: Department Stores (16.9%) and Telecom Services (15.1%) highest; Leisure (10.4%) and Electronic Gaming (8.7%) lowest. Dispersion at industry level significantly wider than sector level.
  • Credit Rating Dominance: Single most powerful driver of WACC. D2/D rated firms: 16.96% vs 11.70% for A1/A+ rated firms. Non-linear relationship — distressed ratings cause exponential increases.
  • Beta & Cost of Equity: Daily beta Ke ≈ 12.4%, Weekly Ke ≈ 13.4%, Monthly Ke ≈ 12.6%. Beta frequency materially alters WACC estimates for the same company.

Limitations & Next Steps

  • Study uses synthetic credit ratings derived from Interest Coverage Ratio — not agency ratings
  • Tax rate not individually adjusted per company for after-tax cost of debt
  • Future: sector-wise visualizations and heatmaps for pattern identification
  • Track WACC changes over time for longitudinal analysis
